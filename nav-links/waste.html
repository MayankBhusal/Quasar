♻️ How to Manage Waste

Proper waste management is crucial to keeping Tilottama clean and green. Here are some effective methods for handling different types of waste:

🍃 Organic Waste

Organic waste includes food scraps, garden waste, and biodegradable materials. Proper disposal helps in composting and reducing landfill usage.

🍃 Organic Waste - How to Manage

  • Use compost bins for food scraps.
  • Keep organic waste separate from plastics and metals.
  • Regularly maintain compost piles to ensure proper decomposition.
  • Consider using kitchen scraps for gardening.
  • Encourage neighbors to compost as well.
  • Participate in community composting programs.

🔄 Recyclable Waste

Recyclable waste consists of materials like paper, cardboard, plastic, and glass that canbe reused after processing.

🔄 Recyclable Waste - How to Manage

  • Sort recyclables into categories: paper, plastic, glass, and metal.
  • Rinse containers before recycling to prevent contamination.
  • Check local recycling programs for proper disposal guidelines.
  • Use reusable bags to reduce plastic waste.
  • Educate others about the importance of recycling.
  • Participate in local recycling drives.

☣️ Hazardous Waste

Hazardous waste includes items like batteries, chemicals, and electronic devices that require special handling to prevent environmental harm.

☣️ Hazardous Waste - How to Manage

  • Dispose of batteries and electronics at designated collection points.
  • Handle chemicals with care and follow disposal instructions.
  • Never mix different types of hazardous waste.
  • Store hazardous materials in secure containers.
  • Contact local authorities for information on hazardous waste disposal.
  • Support initiatives promoting safe hazardous waste management.

🗑️ General Waste

General waste includes non-recyclable and non-hazardous materials that are typically disposed of in landfills.

🗑️ General Waste - How to Manage

  • Reduce the amount of waste you generate.
  • Reuse items whenever possible.
  • Dispose of general waste in designated bins.
  • Avoid using single-use plastics.
  • Support businesses that prioritize waste reduction.
  • Educate yourself about proper waste disposal practices.

📱 Electronic Waste

Electronic waste includes discarded devices such as phones and computers. It should be recycled properly to prevent pollution.

📱 Electronic Waste - How to Manage

  • Donate or sell working electronics instead of discarding them.
  • Take broken devices to certified e-waste recycling centers.
  • Remove personal data before disposing of electronic devices.
  • Participate in e-waste collection events in your community.
  • Educate others about the importance of recycling electronics.
  • Consider repairing devices instead of replacing them.
